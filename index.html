# Web Advertising is Broken Evil advertising practices have become the norm on the web. I'm not saying "evil" just to make a point. I mean **real unethical behavior**. Mainstream sites are trying to pressure and trick you into clicking on things against your wishes. For their own profit. **Evil**. The economics of the web rely too heavily on advertising. Desperate, unsustainable sites will **turn to evil** to stay afloat. They make all kinds of excuses. "Our advertising is helpful" (probably not, but still, no reason to make it invasive). "Users can ignore it if they want" (It takes real time and effort for users to separate real content from ads, especially if you try to confuse them). "My 3rd party advertising service did it, not me" (take your business elsewhere). "This is industry standard." **If jumping off a bridge was industry standard, would your company do it?** # We Can End This It's time to put an end to evil advertising practices. All we have to do is stop tolerating it. **Don't visit evil websites.** Close the tab. Tell them what you think. Call it out. The remainder of this site serves two purposes: to list evil advertising practices and explain what's wrong with them, and to call out major sites that have turned to the dark side. # Don't Use Sites That Do These Things: ## Look At This Ad For X Seconds A user shouldn't have to pay attention to an ad after they already decided they're not interested. ## Interact With This Ad Whether You Like It Or Not Don't make me play a game. Don't make me type "I Love McDonalds" in a CAPTCHA. What you call engaging I call **attempted brainwashing**. ## Look At This Ad Before You See Content Users just look for the close button. It's a waste of time. ## If It Has Two Underlines It's A Trap Don't disguise ads as hyperlinks. Users want to click hyperlinks, users don't want to opt-in to random ads mid-article. These are often called Vibrant Ads because of the company that sells them as "In-Content Contextual Technology." ## Promote Us On Social Media Before You Can Continue I am not willing to treat my recommendation as currency. ## This Looks Like ___ But It's An Ad * Looks like a search result * Looks like a post from my friend * Looks like a post from someone I follow Sites like to mix these in with real content to confuse users. They like to make them look just like the real content. **Saying "Promoted by __" in small gray letters below the ad does not excuse deceitful behavior.** ## Ad That Plays Sound Nobody wants this to happen, ever. ## Ad That Covers Site Content Don't get in the way of people who aren't interested. ## Ad That Is Unreasonably Distracting Same as above. ## Popups / Popunders Plugins and browser improvements have largely killed these, but they're still not gone for good. There is no excuse to use them. # Hall of Shame ## Twitter ![](twitter.png) By the time you get to "Promoted by NetApp" you've already wasted your time reading the ad. # How Do We Fix It? You can advertise without being evil. Google does a good job of this. Their search ads are clearly separated from the search results. The first word in the box is "Ads". Besides that, the market has to change. Actually Paying For Products is on the rise, and this helps reduce the need for advertising. App Stores and Microtransactions will continue to help this trend. It looks many people prefer to pay a few bucks for something rather than take the version with ads. # Ad Block Isn't Enough Ad block doesn't send a strong enough message. If you don't use evil sites, their bottom line will hurt even more. Be strong. Close the tab.
